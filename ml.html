Machine Learning in Air Quality Prediction

Published: March 2026 | Authors: AeroSense Engineering Team | Data Source: OpenAQ

Abstract

This study investigates the efficacy of Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) neural networks in predicting particulate matter (PM2.5) concentrations in urban environments. By leveraging historical data from over 500 monitoring stations, our model achieves significant accuracy in forecasting air quality trends up to 24 hours in advance.

Methodology

The framework utilizes a multi-layered neural network architecture. We integrate meteorological variables such as humidity, wind speed, and temperature from Open-Meteo with real-time pollutant data from OpenAQ. The data is pre-processed using Min-Max scaling to ensure uniform input distribution.

Conclusion

Our findings suggest that machine learning models can serve as a robust early-warning system for urban populations, allowing for proactive health measures during peak pollution events.
